\section{Introduction}

Integral membrane proteins are proteins that cannot be detached from a biological membrane. TODO: BRON (boek) For example they can be found on the outer membrane of bacteria. There are two structural classes of integral membrane proteins:

\begin{itemize}
        \item{$\alpha$-helical}
        \item{$\beta$-barrel}
\end{itemize}

Those two demonstrate different physiochemical characteristics, as well as different structure and localization. BRONBRONBRON Trans membrane segment prediction is well studied when it comes to $\alpha$-helical proteins, but still actively researched on the $\beta$-barrel side. BRONBRONBRON We tried implementing a method to predict the structure of such a $\beta$-barrel protein. This method is based on a hidden Markov model. BRONBRONBRON This method should be capable of predicting the transmembrane beta strands of gram-negative bacteria and discriminating those from water-soluble  proteins in large datastets.

Bacteria can be devided into two groups: Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria. The Gram negative bacteria are distinguished by the fact that they do not retain the crystal violet stain used in the Gram staining method of bacterial differentiation. Their defining characteristic is that their cell envelope consists of a thin peptidoglycan cell wall sandwitched between an inner cytoplasmic membrane and an outer membrane. This outer membrane acts as a protective barrier against numerous antibiotics (including penicillin) and detergents produced by animals as part of their innate immune system. Many pathogens are Gram-negative bacteria.

$\beta$-barrel proteins are often situated in the outer membrane of Gram-negative bacteria. These proteins perform a wide variety of functions such as, active ions transport, passive nutrient uptake, membrane anchoring, adhesion and catalytic activity.

It is no surprise, knowing all of this, that $\beta$-barrel proteins attract an increased medical interest. Transmembrane segment prediciton of $\beta$-barrel proteins would also help us understand how proteins work in general.
